\section{Problem}
(Quadratic forms Q2.66) Find the point at which the line 
$\myvec{-1 & 1}\vec{x}=1$ is a tangent to the curve $y^2=4x$
\section{Solution}
The equation of line is given by 
\begin{align}
    &\myvec{-1 & 1}\vec{x}=1 \\
    &\vec{x}=\myvec{0 \\ 1}+\lambda \myvec{1 \\ 1}=\myvec{\lambda \\ \lambda+1} \label{eq:vector_form}
\end{align}

General equation of a conic is given by
\begin{align}
    \vec{x}^T\vec{Vx}+2\vec{u}^Tx+f=0
\end{align}
for $y^2=4x$, the equation can be written as
\begin{align}
    \vec{x}^T\myvec{0 & 0\\ 0 & 1}\vec{x}+4\myvec{-1 \\ 0}^Tx=0 \label{eq:parabola}
\end{align}
Using \eqref{eq:vector_form} and \eqref{eq:parabola}, we get
\begin{align}
    &\myvec{\lambda \\ \lambda +1}^T\myvec{0 & 0\\ 0 & 1}\myvec{\lambda \\ \lambda +1}-4\myvec{1 \\ 0}^T\myvec{\lambda \\ \lambda+1}=0  \\
    &\myvec{0 & \lambda+1}\myvec{\lambda \\ \lambda +1}-4\lambda=0\\
    &\lambda^2-2\lambda+1=0\\
    &\lambda=1
\end{align}
The point of tangency is given by
\begin{align}
    \vec{x}=\myvec{0 \\ 1}+1\myvec{1 \\ 1}=\myvec{1 \\ 2}
\end{align}
