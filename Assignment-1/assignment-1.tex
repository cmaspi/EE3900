\section{Problem}
Determine if the points
\begin{align}
    \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix},\,
    \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix},\,
    \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ -11 \end{pmatrix}
\end{align}
are collinear

\section{solution}
If the the points are collinear then assuming the points are represented by vectors $\mathbf{A},\, \mathbf{B},\, \mathbf{C}$
then for some $\lambda \in \mathbb{R}-\cbrak{0} $
\begin{align}
    \brak{\mathbf{A-B}} = \lambda \brak{\mathbf{A-C}} \label{eq:condition}
\end{align}

Here
\begin{align}
    &\mathbf{A}=\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix},\\
    &\mathbf{B}=\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix},\\
    &\mathbf{C}=\begin{pmatrix} -2 \\-11 \end{pmatrix}
\end{align}
The vectors 
\begin{align}
    &\mathbf{A-B}=\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix},\\
    &\mathbf{A-C}=\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 16 \end{pmatrix}
\end{align}
Clearly, \eqref{eq:condition} doesn't hold. Therefore, the given points are not collinear.
